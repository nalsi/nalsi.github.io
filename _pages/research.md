My work sits at the intersection of **scholarly communication**, **bibliometrics**, and **quantitative science studies**. I use large-scale bibliometric datasets and computational methods — including network analysis, regression modeling, and natural language processing — to examine how scientific knowledge is produced, organized, and communicated. My research can be organized around three interconnected themes.

---

## Theme 1: A Materialist Understanding of the Scholarly Communication System

In this line of research, I have strived to understand how various research entities (software, datasets, and classification systems) are created, published, used, and shared, and how they affect scientific research.
